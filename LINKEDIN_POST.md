## 🇬🇧 English version

I audited the website of an AI-visibility platform… and found it was invisible on the exact questions its own buyers ask AI assistants.

I turned it into a public case study (client anonymized).

A few findings:

🔹 GEO score 5.5 → 8.8 (potential). Biggest gap: extractability — the pillar that decides whether AI cites you or ignores you.

🔹 87% of Spanish URLs (190 of 219) had English slugs serving Spanish content — a contradictory language signal for AI engines. I built a Python script that flags every mismatch and proposes the correct slug + a 301 redirect map.

🔹 ~103 of 540 URLs returned 404: wasted crawl budget and dead citation targets.

🔹 Strong owned + social citations, but ZERO third-party ones. A Share-of-Voice lead built only on your own domain is fragile — it collapses the moment a competitor earns editorial coverage.

The key insight: performance (Core Web Vitals) was NOT the problem. That let me focus every hour on what actually moves the needle in GEO: semantics, architecture and citable content.

Full methodology (the GEO Gauntlet + the 4 pillars: Entity, Extractability, Evidence, Consensus) and the code are in the repo 👇
