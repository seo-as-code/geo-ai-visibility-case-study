# GEO Gauntlet — Results & Scoreboard

🌐 **[Leer en Español →](./RESULTS.es.md)**

Measured visibility of **AI Visibility Platform A** (anonymized client) inside **ChatGPT**, June 2026. Competitor tools are named as public market context.

> **Protocol:** each of the 51 prompts was run in a fresh chat, memory off, pasting the exact text from a versioned YAML — no extra context added. Outcomes classified as **victory** (cited and/or recommended), **partial** (present, not top pick), **defeat** (absent / clear loser), **ignored** (entity not resolved).

## Where the brand wins

1. **Brand & disambiguation.** With the domain or GEO context in the query, ChatGPT describes the product accurately, cites the domain, and covers its MCP, Share of Voice, action layer and pricing. Wave 1: 8 victory, 1 partial, 1 ignored.
2. **Direct comparisons.** With the brand in the query (*"[Platform A] vs [competitor]"*), the result is favorable in 9 of 11 prompts. Segmentation is usually honest: the brand wins on startups, action workflow and content teams; rivals win on enterprise or pure reporting.
3. **Framework fit.** On the meta-prompt about entity mapping / evidence / consensus / extractability, the brand appears as the first tool aligned to the 4-pillar framework.

## Where the brand loses

1. **Category & ICP shortlists.** On *"best GEO platform for small teams"* or *"B2B tools Europe 2026"*, the brand never makes the final recommendation. Recurring winners: **Peec AI, Goodie AI, AthenaHQ, Frase, SE Ranking**. Wave 2: 0 victory.
2. **How-to & problem-solving.** Ten prompts mapped to the knowledge base (action plan, citation gap, SOV, schema, KB architecture) produced **ten defeats**. ChatGPT returns generic methodology citing no GEO vendor. Wave 4: 0% citation.
3. **Third-party consensus.** Expert ES queries, EU validation and "Semrush/Ahrefs + GEO" stacks omit the brand. Directory presence (G2/Capterra-style) is thin.

## Three operational findings

**1. Two visibility regimes.** Evaluation (brand/vendor in query) → victory/partial. Discovery (generic query, no brand) → defeat. The pre-brand funnel is the bottleneck, not active comparison.

**2. The KB exists; it isn't extracted.** The KB architecture ChatGPT *recommends* for GEO (FAQ, glossary, cases, methodology) matches the client's site — yet no how-to prompt cited it. The content doesn't fail on apparent quality; it fails on **retrieval signal**.

**3. Incomplete social evidence.** The very criteria ChatGPT lists to recommend a GEO platform (reviews, quantified case studies, public pricing, auditable methodology) are only partially covered. Directory listings and ES agency listicles are the most visible gaps.
